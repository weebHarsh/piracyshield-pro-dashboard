# Design Context — PiracyShield Pro

## Product

Content piracy detection and automated takedown SaaS. Monitors streaming sites, torrent indexes, and direct-download hosts for unauthorized copies of protected content. When a match is found, the platform issues DMCA takedown notices automatically.

## Two surfaces, two audiences

### Landing (`/`)
**Audience:** Head of Content Protection, VP of Digital Rights, or Legal Ops lead at a mid-to-large rights holder (studio, record label, sports league). Evaluates the product quarterly against competitors. Has seen every SaaS dashboard demo — knows when something is a template.

**Context:** Vendor comparison. One visit, maybe two. Decision cycle is weeks.

**Job to be done:** Understand in 90 seconds that this is a serious, precise instrument — not another "we use AI" wrapper. They want to see that it handles their actual problem (finding leaks fast and removing them faster) and that the numbers are specific and credible.

**Emotional register:** Confident authority. The product should feel like it was built by people who have done this job themselves — not by engineers who read a DMCA FAQ.

### Dashboard (`/incidents`, `/takedowns`, `/content`, `/overview`, `/users`)
**Audience:** Rights Operations Analyst or Content Protection Engineer. Uses this tool multiple times per day, often during active incidents (a pre-release leaked, a live event is being pirated in real time).

**Context:** Crisis mode is the default. Every extra click, every 400ms animation, every unnecessary visual element increases cognitive load during the exact moments that matter most.

**Job to be done:** Identify the highest-severity active incidents, see status at a glance, act (issue takedown, escalate, whitelist) without friction.

**Emotional register:** Functional calm. Precision over decoration. Everything in service of the next decision.

---

## Shared personality

**Precise.** Numbers are specific, never rounded to the nearest "k" or "M." Timestamps are exact. Status is unambiguous.

**Authoritative.** The product speaks directly. No hedging ("it looks like"), no filler ("seamless," "next-gen"), no startup enthusiasm. Copy that sounds like it was written by someone who has been in a rights dispute.

**Unhurried.** The landing page does not beg for attention. The dashboard does not distract with motion. Both surfaces let the content do the work.

---

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS admin templates (Tremor, Metabase, Retool demos)
- Draggable KPI dashboards (novelty with no ops value)
- "AI purple/blue" gradient branding
- 3-equal-column card grids with icons above headings
- Gradient text on headlines
- Glow borders and outer-glow shadows
- Any product that looks like it was built from a Next.js shadcn starter in a weekend

## References

- **Linear** — typographic precision, table density, no-wasted-pixel layout
- **Stripe Docs** — authority through specificity; copy that respects the reader's expertise
- **Raycast** — command palette, instant interactions, nothing animated that doesn't need to be
- **Height** — data UI motion budget (almost zero, everything functional)
- **Klim Type Foundry** — restraint as a typographic value

## Typography

- **PP Editorial New** (Light / Ultralight / Ultralight Italic) — landing display H1/H2 only. Editorial confidence.
- **PP Neue Montreal** (Book / Medium / Bold) — body and UI copy on both surfaces. Mechanical, confident.
- **PP Fraktion Mono** (Regular / Medium) — every number on both surfaces: KPIs, table cells, chart labels, timestamps, IDs.

All Pangram Pangram. Free for commercial use.

---

## Color

One brand accent (teal). Five semantic status levels (critical / high / medium / low / info). All OKLCH. Tinted neutrals.

No gradients on text. No colored glow shadows. No hue outside brand + status on any screen.

---

## Motion rules

**Landing:** Generous entrance budget. Scroll reveals are the signature — not looping animations. One well-orchestrated load > scattered micro-interactions.

**Dashboard:** Stingy budget. Route transitions ≤ 150ms. No entrance staggers on data. No hover lifts. Every animation that doesn't directly communicate state change should be removed.

Both surfaces: always gate on `prefers-reduced-motion`.
